Problem Overview and Goal

Many consumers do not have the time or access to information about toxicity of both inert and active ingredients in the products they buy.

Our aim with this project is to empower consumers with information about toxic ingredients used in products. This will enable them to take better informed decisions while buying products.

User Interview

We conducted 10 interviews with people of defferent age groups, to understand the following:

1. How consumers get information about products ?

2. Attitude towards the environment.

3. How consumers react to news about products that are toxic to the environment or to people ?

4. Motivations towards learning about the toxicity level of products.

5. How comfortable consumers are to change products that they have been using ?

Interview Insights

By analyzing the interviews we discovered the following insights :

  1. Effect of toxins on the environment is not the key factor when it comes to buying products because safe products are usually expensive.

    Many interviewees indicated that the cost factor related to products was more important than the potential harmful impact of those products on the environment. Consumers expressed that they would be more concerned about the toxicity of products if they impacted them directly.

    2. Supporting quotes on Insight 1

  2. Flexibility to change products.

    Our interviewees indicated that they would be comfortable switching products that they have been using since a long time. If the new product is better for the environment and has a similar cost then they would switch products.

    3. Supporting quotes on Insight 2

  3. Reliable sources of information include friends, family, influencers and, scientific explainations.

    Through our interviews we realized that users did not easily believe rumors about products. Users trusted information they received from their friends, family, inner social circle. They did not believe rumors about products without research.

    4. Supporting quotes on Insight 3

  4. Limited participation by consumers in discussion forums when compared to social media apps and word of mouth.

    This was a surprising find, both interviewees said that in spite of consuming and contributing to information on discussion forums, they would not share product related information on a discussion forum.

    This led us to consider that users might not be comfortable sharing information with unknown people on discussion forums but they are quite comfortable to convey their opinions on social media where they have an established circle of friends.

    5. Supporting quotes on Insight 4

Competitive Analysis

We conducted competitive analysis to better understand the problem space.Based on the pain points of our personas. We identified the following competitors for analysis:

1. Target Store App - Partial Competitor

2. Change.org - Parallel Competitor

3. Think Dirty - Analogous Competitor

We identified pros and cons in these applications that we could use as inspiration for Beyond Barcode. Following are some pros that we used as inspiration for Beyond Barcode Features.

Target Store App

Partial Competitor

Pros:

  1. Price scanning functionality for items, for in-store price checking convenience that helps consumers compare multiple products at once.
  2. The location function makes searching for available items in your store easier and provides alternatives if your preferred item is not immediately available.
  3. Provides a product description tab and item highlights tab for all items. These pages provide a breakdown of ingredients and the item benefits for consumers.
  4. There is a user review tab that also allows customers to read the experiences of other buyers. This tab also includes #TargetStyle as a social media hashtag for consumers to find products.

Change.org

Parallel Competitor

Pros:

  1. Anyone can start a petition, the platform is open to everyone.
  2. There is media coverage and offline events to reach new audiences. This is an impactful step in creating a community since it gives people the opportunity to connect beyond the online platform.
  3. Each petition shows the current number of supporters and a progress bar. Complete progress bars would get reactions from decision makers. Victories have information related to the impact created by respective petitions.
  4. Every petition has a “featured” section that has links to other articles about that issue. This is useful to indicate the intensity of issues.

Think Dirty

Analogous Competitor

Pros:

  1. Users will get an overall score for the product, and a list of ingredients ranking from the most harmful 10 score to the cleanest 0 score with striking colors to separate them, except for a few not in the database.
  2. The homepage contains all different types of personal used products, and all of them are clean. The ultimate goal for users is to buy some clean products, so with this homepage, they can easily buy them without even going to stores to scan each of them.

Problem

After qualitative data analysis we decided to address the following problems through our solution:

1. Inform users about toxic ingredients in compounds.

2. Build a platform that consumers can trust.

3. Make users feel as if they contributed towards making a change.

Key Features

Based on rapid ideation and sketching, we came up with key functionalities that our app would provide.

Problem 1

To inform users about toxic ingredients present in products.

Feature

We designed a scanning feature that would scan barcodes and give information about products to users.

Beyond Barcode has scores for products that are calculated by considering Health and Environmental Factors.

Product Scanning and product page with rating.

Adding a product and product page with reviews.

Problem 2

To earn users’ trust on the information provided by Beyond Barcode.

Feature

In order to earn users’ trust, we decided to have user generated content in the application. This would make them feel that consumers like them are contributing to information. Consumers could participate by:

  1. Adding products that do not exist to the Beyond Barcode database.
  2. Reviewing products that they have had ecperience with.

Problem 3

To make users feel empowered.

Feature

We designed a petition functionality that would connect users with decision makers.

Consumers can start and sign petitions about products. This would make them feel empowered and give them a sense of contribution.

Feedback from paper prototype

We tested the paper prototype to get feedback from users about the design. For this purpose we assigned the following task to our users:

Task: To find rating about a product and mark the selected product as favourite.

Feedback 1

Users tried to find information about products by using the search function. The scan functionality was not prominent on the homepage.

Feedback from Low-Fi prototype

We tested the low fi prototype to get feedback from users about the design. For this purpose we assigned the following task to our users:

Task: To add a new product to the database from the application, fill a petition for a toxic product and share it with friends.

Feedback 1

Users did not understand the meaning of featured products and were not aware of the petition feature.

Solution

We removed featured products from the homepage and added popular petitions there. This would let users know that they can add/sign petitions through the app.

We also added popular products on the Homepage with higher ratings.

Achievements

This project was a part of my Interaction Design course and we were awarded Best Design in a class of 75 students. My teammates and I currently building on this project for Innovation in Action hosted by the Center for Socially Engaged Design at the University of Michigan.

Takeaway

Importance of qualitative research

We used a variety of qualitative research methods which led to the discovery of the problem statement. This project made me realize the importance of qualitative research methods and how they can be used to clearly understand the problem space before designing solutions.

Iterative Feedback on designs

We conducted multiple usability tests with users at each stage of the design process. This led us to discover surprising insights and indicated that our assumptions were not always correct.
